# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of this project, part of the **NIT Raipur Open Source Organization**, pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### ✅ Examples of Behavior That Contributes to a Positive Environment

- **Being respectful** of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- **Giving and gracefully accepting** constructive feedback
- **Accepting responsibility** for mistakes and learning from them
- **Focusing on what is best** for the community and the project
- **Showing empathy** towards other community members
- **Using welcoming and inclusive language**
- **Being patient** with new contributors and helping them learn
- **Celebrating others' contributions** and successes

### ❌ Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

- The use of **sexualized language or imagery**, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
- **Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments**, and personal or political attacks
- **Public or private harassment** of any kind
- Publishing others' **private information** (physical or email addresses) without explicit permission
- **Spamming** issues or pull requests with irrelevant content
- **Plagiarism** or claiming others' work as your own
- Submitting **deliberately malicious code** or security vulnerabilities
- **Dismissing or attacking** someone for their level of experience or knowledge
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered **inappropriate in a professional or educational setting**

---

## Our Responsibilities

### Maintainers

Project maintainers are responsible for:
- Clarifying and enforcing standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action in response to behavior violations
- Removing, editing, or rejecting comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct
- Temporarily or permanently banning contributors for behaviors deemed inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful

### Core Team

The core team of the NIT Raipur OSS Organization will:
- Support maintainers in enforcing the Code of Conduct
- Handle appeals and escalated violations
- Review serious violations with faculty trustees when necessary
- Ensure fair and consistent application of these standards

### Faculty Trustees

Faculty trustees serve as final arbiters in serious violations and:
- Review core team decisions when appealed
- Make binding decisions in cases of serious misconduct
- Ensure institutional compliance and protect the organization's integrity

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces managed by this project, including:

- GitHub repositories (issues, PRs, discussions, code)
- Communication channels (Discord, Slack, email lists)
- In-person events (contribution drives, meetups, workshops)
- Social media accounts related to the project
- Any other forum representing the project or community

It also applies when an individual is representing the project or community in public spaces. Examples include using an official project email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

## Enforcement Process

### Investigation

1. **Report received** - Acknowledged within 24 hours
2. **Initial review** - Maintainers/core team assess severity
3. **Investigation** - Gather context from all parties involved
4. **Decision** - Determine appropriate action based on guidelines

### Consequences

Violations will result in consequences proportional to the severity and context:

#### 1. Correction (Minor Violations)

**Examples:** Inappropriate language, minor unprofessional behavior

**Action:**
- Private written warning
- Explanation of why behavior was inappropriate
- Request for public apology (if applicable)

#### 2. Warning (Moderate Violations)

**Examples:** Pattern of inappropriate behavior, dismissive comments, spam

**Action:**
- Formal written warning
- Temporary restriction from community spaces (1-7 days)
- No interaction with people involved for specified period
- Violation noted in records

#### 3. Temporary Ban (Serious Violations)

**Examples:** Harassment, sustained inappropriate behavior, deliberate intimidation

**Action:**
- Temporary ban from all community spaces (7-90 days)
- No interaction with community during ban period
- Public announcement (if necessary for community safety)
- Formal record of violation

#### 4. Permanent Ban (Severe Violations)

**Examples:** Sexual harassment, threats of violence, doxxing, sustained harassment, repeat offenses

**Action:**
- Permanent ban from all community spaces
- Public announcement with details (privacy-respecting)
- Report to college administration (if involving students)
- Legal action (if criminal behavior)

### Appeals

If you believe you were unjustly sanctioned:

1. Submit an appeal within **7 days** of the decision
2. Appeals go to the next level:
   - Maintainer decisions → Core Team
   - Core Team decisions → Faculty Trustees
   - Faculty Trustee decisions → College Administration
3. Appeals are reviewed within **14 days**
4. Decision on appeal is final

## Enforcement Guidelines

Decision-makers will consider these questions when determining consequences:

### Impact
- Who was affected?
- How severe was the impact?
- Was it a one-time incident or pattern?

### Intent
- Was the behavior intentional or accidental?
- Did the person apologize and show willingness to learn?
- Is there a history of similar behavior?

### Context
- Was this during high-stress period (exams, deadlines)?
- Were there cultural or language misunderstandings?
- Is the person a minor or new to community norms?

### Community Safety
- Is the person a danger to others?
- Would removal protect the community?
- Can the behavior be corrected?

## Positive Community Building

Beyond enforcing rules, we encourage:

### Mentorship
- Senior students helping juniors
- Experienced contributors guiding newcomers
- Maintainers providing constructive feedback

### Recognition
- Celebrating contributions (code, docs, community support)
- Highlighting positive behaviors
- Thanking people publicly

### Education
- Workshops on inclusive language
- Resources on respectful communication
- Learning from mistakes together

### Inclusivity
- Welcoming contributors of all skill levels
- Providing multiple ways to contribute (code, docs, design, community)
- Accommodating different learning styles and needs

## Special Considerations

### Academic Environment

As a college-based organization:
- Respect that students balance academics with contributions
- Be understanding during exam periods
- Encourage learning over perfection
- Remember that students are still developing professionally

### Contribution Drives

During fest events:
- Competition should remain friendly and respectful
- No sabotage of others' contributions
- Help others learn, don't hoard knowledge
- Maintainers remain impartial in reviews

### Cultural Diversity

NIT Raipur is diverse:
- Be patient with language barriers
- Avoid jokes that may not translate culturally
- Respect different communication styles
- Ask for clarification rather than assuming offense
